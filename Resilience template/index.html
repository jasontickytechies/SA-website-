How to Build Resilience?

What is resilience?

Resilience, by definition, means having the ability to withstand adversity and effectively bounce back from complex life events and come out on top. Resilience does not necessarily mean that a person did not experience strength, emotional upheaval, and suffering. Instead, resilience focuses on working through emotional pain and suffering.

Factors of resilience?

Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card's content.

Being able to master the skill of resilience can really give you a huge edge in life. So understanding its factors and what impacts it has can give you a better understanding of how to build and maintain it.

  • having the capacity to make achievable, realistic plans
  • being able to carry out those plans
  • Healthy ways of managing and manipulating your feelings and impulses.
  • Building and improving communication skills
  • Belief in your own strength and ability

How to build resilience?

Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card's content.

Building resilience is far harder than you could imagine. Not only do you have to face it, but you will also be dealing with the emotional burdens at the same time when you deal with your situation. Fortunately, there are many ways to improve your resilience.

Get Connections

First, get connected with many strong, positive relationships with your friends and loved ones. Having access to an environment that supports you makes it that much easier to overcome your challenges.

Learn from experience

Whether you have faced an event you needed to be resilient for or you did not quite fully recover from it, Learn about it and analyze every aspect of it. Understand what went wrong and what could have gone better. If you are still confused, ask from a different perspective from a more experienced and knowledgeable person.

Remain hopeful and take care of yourself

The last thing you would want when a catastrophic life event happens is to be unable to believe that there can be a scenario that is worth living. When everything you do matters, having hope and taking good care of yourself along the way will help to make the recovery process go more smoothly.

Be proactive

Finally, be proactive. when you are not currently confronted with major challenges, issues, or unfortunate events Drill yourself every day, go into a thought experiment, brainstorm reactions and contingency plans, and do independent research on what to do in case anything happens that causes you to adapt and change your entire lifestyle.
